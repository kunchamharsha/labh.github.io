Formula

$$ A = P \times \left( \frac{(1 + r)^n - 1}{r} \right) $$

A Total corpus at retirement (maturity amount)
P Contribution amount per month
r Expected rate of return per month (Annual return ÷ 12 ÷ 100)
n Total number of contributions (Years × 12)

FAQ's

What is the minimum contribution for NPS?

The minimum annual contribution is ₹1,000 for Tier-I accounts.

C Can I withdraw my NPS corpus before retirement?

Partial withdrawals are allowed after 3 years for specific purposes like education, marriage, or medical treatment.

Is NPS taxable at maturity?

60% of the corpus is tax-free, while the remaining 40% must be used to purchase an annuity, which is taxable.
